BRIGHT SMILE DENTAL STUDIO — FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: Do you accept emergency walk-ins?
A: We reserve same-day slots for urgent issues during business hours. Calling ahead helps us triage pain or swelling and prepare the right instruments. True emergencies (difficulty breathing, uncontrolled bleeding, facial trauma) should go to the nearest ER or call emergency services.

Q: Is teeth whitening safe for sensitive teeth?
A: Many patients with mild sensitivity can still whiten safely with adjusted protocols, desensitizing gel, and shorter sessions. We assess enamel and gum health first—over-the-counter kits are not always appropriate.

Q: How often should I get a cleaning?
A: Most adults with healthy gums benefit from a professional cleaning every six months. If you have gum disease or risk factors, we may recommend every three to four months—this is medical, not “upselling.”

Q: Do you offer sedation?
A: We provide local anesthesia and nitrous oxide (laughing gas) for qualifying cases. IV sedation and general anesthesia are not offered in-office; we refer out when deeper sedation is needed.

Q: Can I bring my child to my appointment?
A: Young children are welcome in the waiting area with supervision. For clinical safety and focus, only the scheduled patient should be in the operatory unless discussed in advance.

Q: What if I am anxious about dental visits?
A: Tell us—many people feel the same way. We can schedule longer appointments, explain each step, use noise-canceling headphones, and discuss nitrous oxide. Small wins build trust over time.

Q: Do you replace silver (amalgam) fillings?
A: Yes, when they fail, crack, or when you prefer tooth-colored restorations for visible teeth. We do not remove sound amalgam solely for cosmetic reasons without discussing risks, benefits, and alternatives.

Q: How long do dental implants last?
A: Implants can last many years with good hygiene and regular maintenance. Longevity depends on bone quality, bite forces, smoking, and gum health—similar to natural teeth in many ways.

Q: Why do I need X-rays?
A: We cannot see decay between teeth, bone loss, or infections under the gumline without them. We follow ALARA principles (as low as reasonably achievable) and use digital sensors to minimize exposure.

Q: How do I transfer records from another office?
A: Sign a release form (we provide a template), and we will request charts and images from your previous dentist. Allow 5–10 business days for records to arrive.
